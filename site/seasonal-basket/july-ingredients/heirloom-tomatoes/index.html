July · San Diego

Heirloom tomatoes

July is peak tomato season in Southern California. The heat concentrates their sugars and the variety is at its widest. This is the month they deserve the center of the plate.

How to choose it

  • Heavy for their size
  • Fragrant at the stem end
  • Give slightly under gentle pressure
  • Irregular shape is a good sign, not a flaw
  • Avoid anything refrigerated at the store

Buy this much: 4–6 medium tomatoes for one person for the week

Three directions

Italian Summer
Olive oil, basil, garlic, black pepper, flaky salt.
Bright Taco
Lime, cilantro, corn, cucumber, a little heat.
Simple Toast
Sourdough, olive oil, salt. The tomato does the rest.

On a weekday

  • Rice bowl topping with basil
  • Chicken thigh salad with cucumber
  • Pasta sauce with garlic and olive oil
  • Toast with salt and olive oil

On a weekend

Thick slices with burrata, basil, olive oil, and sourdough for dragging.

One thing worth learning

Smell the stem end before you buy. A tomato that smells like a tomato at the store will taste like one at home.

When a tomato is right, the knife almost does it on its own.
— Rosa, The tomato grower

Ask at the market: Which variety do you think is best this week?

Good with this month

Basil, Persian cucumbers, Sweet corn, Peaches

Good with your staples

chicken thighs, ground beef or turkey, salmon, beans, sourdough

Storage

Counter only. Never refrigerate. Use within four or five days.
