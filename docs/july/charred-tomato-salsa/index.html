Charred Tomato Salsa

Same tomato. Different life.

Make one jar

  • 3 ripe tomatoes, halved
  • ½ white onion
  • 1 garlic clove, unpeeled
  • Flaky salt
  • ½ lime
  • Optional: ½ serrano, stem removed

Set a dry cast-iron pan or comal over high heat until very hot. Add the tomatoes cut-side down, the onion, the garlic, and the optional serrano. Leave them alone. Let them char — dark spots are the point. When the tomatoes have softened and the onion and garlic are charred in places, pull everything off. Squeeze the garlic from its skin. Rough-chop everything together or pulse briefly in a blender. Season with salt and a squeeze of lime. Leave it thick.

Refrigerate. Gets better after an hour.

Use it this week

Tacos Spoon it on.

Works with beef, fish, or beans. Let it be the sauce.

Beans Stir it in.

Add a few spoonfuls while the beans are still warm. They'll absorb it.

Fish Serve alongside.

Particularly good with salmon or whatever's freshest at the counter.

Eggs Scramble it in.

Add a spoonful to the pan when the eggs are half-set.

Made with

  • Heirloom tomatoes

From Field Notes

  • Flaky salt goes on last
  • Ask what's freshest.
