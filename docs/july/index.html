Field Notes

Sherry vinegar

Tomatoes are already acidic. That's why sharp vinegar fights them — you're adding the same note twice. Sherry vinegar is rounder, with a quiet sweetness of its own. It cooperates. Try it on sliced tomatoes instead of lemon. A splash in the pan after chicken. A few drops in cucumber water. You'll notice immediately, and you won't go back.

Flaky salt goes on last

Table salt dissolves into food. Flaky salt stays on the surface, so you taste it in distinct moments rather than uniformly throughout. On a ripe tomato or a peach, that difference is the whole experience. Keep a small dish of it near where you plate. It only goes on last — never while cooking.

Add herbs after the heat

Basil and mint lose what makes them interesting the moment heat reaches them. The oils that carry the fragrance are gone within seconds of contact with a hot pan. Add them after the heat is off, or raw, right before the plate reaches the table. The smell alone will tell you something has changed.

The weekend meal

Tomato, burrata, basil, and sourdough.

One meal should feel like the reason you went shopping. This is July’s.

Use
  • 2 heirloom tomatoes
  • 1 ball burrata
  • Basil
  • Olive oil
  • Flaky salt and black pepper
  • Toasted sourdough
Do

Slice the tomatoes thickly. Tear open the burrata. Add basil, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Use warm sourdough to drag through everything left on the plate.

One thing to notice

The smell of a tomato vine.

Most people know what a tomato tastes like. Fewer notice the green, almost peppery scent where the stem meets the fruit.

The drink

Hillcrest Spritz

July's house drink. Make it on repeat.

Keep
  • 2 oz tart cherry juice
  • Sparkling mineral water
  • Ice
July adds
  • ½ lime
  • 6–8 mint leaves
Method

Fill a rocks glass with ice. Add cherry juice and lime. Slap the mint once between your palms, drop it in, then top with sparkling water. Add more sparkling water to taste.

Try another way
Use basil instead of mint If basil is already out for dinner.
Add a few cucumber slices If there's half a cucumber left.
Muddle a ripe peach slice with the lime If the peaches are especially ripe.
Add more sparkling water If you want something lighter.
San Diego ritual

Take dinner outside.

Pack tomatoes, bread, fruit, and something cold to drink. Eat near the water while the light is still hanging around.
