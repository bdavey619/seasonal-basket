July · San Diego

Blackberries

Wild and cultivated blackberries both peak in July in Southern California. They are deeply flavored, slightly tart, and good with almost nothing added.

How to choose it

  • Deep, uniform black with no red druplets remaining
  • Plump but not leaking
  • Check the bottom of the container for mold
  • A little give without being mushy

Buy this much: One pint. Use within two or three days.

July is peak season — pints that cost $4–5 off-season often drop to $2–3. One of the best deals of the summer.

On a weekday

  • Greek yogurt topping in the morning
  • Eaten as a snack
  • Added to a fruit plate with peaches and mint

On a weekend

Fruit plate with peaches, mint, and honey as a light dessert.

One thing worth learning

Blackberries are fragile. They do not improve with time. Buy them close to when you will eat them and treat the container gently.

Good with this month

Mint, Peaches, Cherries

Good with your staples

greek yogurt, sourdough

Storage

Refrigerate unwashed and use within two to three days. Wash only just before eating.
