July · San Diego

Basil

Basil is at its most fragrant in warm weather. July heat brings out the volatile oils. The best basil right now smells like summer itself.

How to choose it

  • Bright, even green leaves — no dark spots
  • Fragrant when you lean in
  • Stems still firm
  • Avoid wilted or yellowing bunches

Buy this much: One or two good bunches. It moves through a week faster than you expect.

On a weekday

  • Torn over rice bowls
  • Added to tomato and cucumber salads
  • Stirred into pasta with olive oil and garlic
  • Layered on a tomato toast

On a weekend

Torn generously over burrata and tomatoes with good olive oil.

One thing worth learning

Tear basil instead of chopping when it will be served fresh. The knife bruises it and darkens it quickly. Torn leaves hold their color and fragrance longer.

Good with this month

Heirloom tomatoes, Persian cucumbers, Sweet corn, Peaches

Good with your staples

chicken thighs, ground beef or turkey, salmon, beans, sourdough

Storage

Keep at room temperature with stems in water, like flowers. Do not refrigerate. Use within three or four days.
