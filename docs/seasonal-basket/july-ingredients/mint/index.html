Mint

Mint is cooling by nature and perfect in summer heat. A few leaves change the temperature of a dish or a drink entirely.

How to choose it

  • Perky, upright leaves — not limp
  • Deep green without yellowing
  • Strongly fragrant when a leaf is pressed
  • Spearmint or peppermint both work for this edition

Buy this much: One bunch. It goes further than you think.

One of the least expensive things in this basket — usually $1–2 a bunch at any grocery store.

During the week

  • Cucumber and mint side salad
  • Torn over yogurt with fruit
  • Added to a salmon rice bowl
  • Stirred into sparkling water with lime

On a weekend

In the Hillcrest Spritz. Or over a peach and blackberry plate with honey.

One thing worth learning

Slapping a mint leaf between your palms before using it releases the oils without bruising the leaf. It is worth doing.

Good with this month

Persian cucumbers, Peaches, Cherries, Blackberries

Good with your staples

greek yogurt, salmon, chicken thighs, sticky rice

Storage

Keep in water at room temperature or wrapped loosely in a damp paper towel in the refrigerator. Use within five days.
