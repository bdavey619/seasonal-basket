# July Edition Brief — San Diego

## Editorial Thesis

July is the month when tomatoes deserve the center of the plate.

Cucumbers make everything cooler.

Peaches need almost nothing.

The user's usual meals can stay exactly as simple as they are—only now they taste like July.

## Featured Ingredients

1. Heirloom tomatoes
2. Persian cucumbers
3. Peaches
4. Sweet corn
5. Basil
6. Mint
7. Cherries
8. Blackberries

## Seasonal Basket

The basket should support:

- Five weekday lunches
- Five weekday dinners
- Two weekend meals
- Light breakfasts using fruit and yogurt
- Repetition across ingredients
- Low waste risk

## Staple Meal Integration

### Ground beef or turkey

- Tomato and basil rice bowl
- Corn, beans, and lime taco bowl
- Chili-crisp cucumber bowl

### Chicken thighs

- Italian-seasoned chicken with tomato salad
- Taco-seasoned chicken with corn and beans
- Soy-marinated chicken with cucumber and chili crisp

### Salmon

- Salmon and cucumber rice bowl
- Salmon with tomato and basil salad
- Salmon with corn and peach salsa

### Beans

- Tomato, cucumber, basil, and bean salad
- Corn and bean taco bowl
- White beans with tomato and sourdough

## Flavor Paths

### Italian Summer

Italian seasoning, garlic, basil, tomato, olive oil, black pepper.

### Bright Taco

Taco seasoning, lime, corn, tomato, cucumber, beans.

### Chili-Crisp Bowl

Soy or sesame marinade, chili crisp, cucumber, corn, basil or mint.

## Weekend Meal

Tomato, burrata, basil, olive oil, flaky salt, black pepper, and toasted sourdough.

## Drink

Hillcrest Spritz:

- 2 oz tart cherry juice
- 1/2 lime
- Mint
- Sparkling mineral water
- Ice

## One Thing to Notice

The green, peppery smell where the tomato stem meets the fruit.
